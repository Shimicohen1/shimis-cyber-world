The shift to post-quantum cryptography (PQC) is a significant undertaking for the cybersecurity industry, and Cisco is laying out its strategy for its Secure Firewall products. Pentesting News highlights Cisco's proactive approach to this cryptographic transition, which aims to protect against future quantum computing threats.

Cisco's roadmap focuses on integrating PQC algorithms into their firewall solutions. This involves careful consideration of performance impacts and ensuring backward compatibility where necessary. The company is working towards a future where their firewalls can effectively defend networks against attacks that could break current encryption standards, a critical step for long-term security infrastructure.

Pentesting News points out that this move is essential as quantum computers mature. While widespread quantum decryption capabilities are not yet a reality, the transition to PQC is a marathon, not a sprint. Cisco's commitment signals a broader industry trend towards future-proofing security architectures against emerging, powerful threats.
