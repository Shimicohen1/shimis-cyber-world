Schools across Northern Ireland are reeling from a significant cyberattack that has crippled the Education Authority's IT systems. Pentesting News reported on the incident, which has led to widespread disruption for students, teachers, and administrative staff. The exact nature and scope of the attack are still under investigation, but the immediate impact has been severe, forcing the closure of some services and hindering essential operations.

This incident highlights the persistent vulnerability of public sector organizations to cyber threats. The reliance on digital infrastructure for everything from student records to online learning means that successful attacks can have far-reaching consequences. Pentesting News noted that the disruption is affecting various aspects of the education system, underscoring the critical need for robust cybersecurity measures within these vital institutions.
