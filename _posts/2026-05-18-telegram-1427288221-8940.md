The Israeli banking sector's C-suite is, predictably, most concerned about cyber risk heading into 2025. This isn't a surprise; it's a reflection of the unrelenting pressure financial institutions face from sophisticated threat actors.

According to Cyber News - Erez Dasa, this finding comes from the newly published 2025 report by the Supervisor of Banks for the Israeli banking system. The report details risk rankings starting on page 85, with a dedicated section on cyber risk commencing on page 131.

This isn't just about compliance. It’s about the very real, tangible impact of a breach on financial stability, customer trust, and national security. The banking sector remains a prime target, and the regulatory acknowledgment of cyber as the paramount risk underscores the critical need for robust, proactive defense strategies.
