A recent survey highlighted by Cyber News - Erez Dasa reveals a stark reality: Israeli users, much like their global counterparts, consistently opt for easily guessable passwords. This isn't a unique national flaw; it's a pervasive human one, especially among non-technical individuals.

Erez Dasa rightly points out that this trend is unlikely to change as long as passwords remain a primary authentication method. The inherent friction and cognitive load of managing strong, unique passwords are simply too much for the average user, creating an irresistible attack surface for adversaries.

The strategic imperative for CISOs is clear: accelerate the transition away from traditional passwords. Services with robust security postures have long moved to biometric and other passwordless authentication methods. Relying on user diligence for password strength is a losing battle; the attacker's calculus is simple – exploit the weakest link, which consistently proves to be human behavior around credentials.
