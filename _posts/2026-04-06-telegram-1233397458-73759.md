Phishing emails remain the primary vector for cyberattacks, with a staggering 75% of all malicious campaigns originating from them, according to a recent statement by the UAE Cyber Council. This statistic underscores a persistent and deeply entrenched threat that continues to exploit human vulnerability. The sheer volume of these attacks highlights how effective, albeit low-tech, social engineering tactics can be in breaching even sophisticated defenses.

The sheer persistence of phishing as an attack vector is a testament to its effectiveness. Attackers leverage a mix of urgency, authority, and deception to trick unsuspecting individuals into divulging sensitive information or executing malicious code. This tactic bypasses many technical controls, making user awareness and robust security training absolutely critical components of any organization's defense strategy.
