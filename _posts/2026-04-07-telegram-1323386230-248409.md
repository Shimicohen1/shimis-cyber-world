Cyber Threat Intelligence has flagged a concerning increase in cyber fraud targeting individuals of Indian origin within the United States. This surge appears to be directly correlated with the heightened immigration enforcement and crackdown initiated under the Trump administration. Scammers are reportedly exploiting the anxieties and uncertainties surrounding immigration status to perpetrate various fraudulent schemes.

These scams often prey on individuals' fear of deportation or legal repercussions, coercing them into divulging sensitive personal information or making illicit payments. Cyber Threat Intelligence's findings suggest a sophisticated, albeit malicious, use of social engineering tactics, leveraging the current political climate to build a false sense of urgency and authority. This tactic is particularly effective against vulnerable communities who may be less familiar with their rights or more susceptible to threats related to their immigration status.
