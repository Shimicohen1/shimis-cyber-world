Cyber Threat Intelligence is shedding light on a massive $280 million cryptocurrency theft, revealing it was the culmination of a meticulous, six-month in-person operation. The breach targeted the Drift Protocol, a decentralized finance (DeFi) platform, and highlights a disturbing trend where sophisticated actors are moving beyond purely digital attacks to incorporate real-world reconnaissance and social engineering.

Details from Cyber Threat Intelligence suggest the attackers didn't just exploit a smart contract vulnerability. Instead, they spent half a year physically infiltrating or gathering intel on individuals associated with the protocol. This suggests a deep dive into operational security, potentially involving physical access, insider threats, or highly targeted phishing campaigns that leveraged real-world interactions. The sheer scale of the theft, coupled with the protracted, hands-on approach, underscores the evolving tactics of high-level cybercriminals targeting the DeFi space.
