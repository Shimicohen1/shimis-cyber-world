Polish intelligence has issued a stark warning regarding cyberattacks targeting critical water treatment control systems. The Record by Recorded Future reports that while no specific group or country has been publicly attributed to these incidents, Poland anticipates a significant uptick in hostile cyber activity through 2024 and 2025, with a pointed emphasis on Russian Federation special services.

This isn't just about data theft; it's about operational disruption. Hitting water treatment infrastructure indicates a clear intent to impact civilian life and critical services. Attackers aren't just looking for a quick payout; they're aiming for strategic destabilization. This type of targeting underscores the evolving calculus of state-sponsored threat actors, moving beyond traditional espionage to directly threaten public safety.

For defenders, this means reassessing the security posture of all critical infrastructure, especially SCADA and ICS environments. Relying on network segmentation alone is no longer sufficient. These systems need continuous monitoring, robust anomaly detection, and a clear incident response plan that accounts for physical as well as cyber consequences. Assume compromise and build resilience.
