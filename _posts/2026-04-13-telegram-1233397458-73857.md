The connected home is now extending into the bedroom, with smart sex toys entering the market. While these devices offer enhanced features and remote control capabilities, Pentesting News highlights the significant privacy and security risks they introduce. These aren't about remote hacking of your vibrator, but rather the more common threats of data breaches and tracking.

Companion apps for these devices often go beyond basic controls. They can include social features like private messaging, group chats, and even video sessions, effectively turning some services into niche dating platforms. The data flow involves Bluetooth connections between the toy and the phone, and then to cloud servers for social features and remote access. This constant data exchange, as reported by Pentesting News, presents a substantial privacy exposure.

Pentesting News emphasizes that the risks are real, stemming from the highly personal data these apps can collect. Users need to be aware of what information is being transmitted and stored, and take steps to mitigate potential privacy headaches associated with these intimate gadgets.
