Cyber Threat Intelligence is reporting a potentially colossal data breach originating from China's National Supercomputing Center. The source claims over 10 petabytes of sensitive military and aerospace data were exfiltrated by a hacker. While details remain unconfirmed, the sheer volume and nature of the alleged stolen data, if true, could have significant ripple effects on international relations and national security.

This incident, if verified, highlights the persistent threat landscape faced by critical national infrastructure, even highly advanced ones. The implications extend beyond mere data theft; the potential weaponization of such highly sensitive information by adversaries could destabilize geopolitical balances. This event also underscores the ongoing challenges in securing massive data repositories, especially those with ties to sensitive sectors like defense and aerospace.
