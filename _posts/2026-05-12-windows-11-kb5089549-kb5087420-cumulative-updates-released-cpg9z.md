Microsoft has rolled out cumulative updates KB5089549 and KB5087420 for Windows 11 versions 25H2/24H2 and 23H2, respectively. BleepingComputer reports that these updates address critical security vulnerabilities, squash various bugs, and introduce new features. Staying current with these patches is non-negotiable for maintaining a robust security posture.

These updates are more than just bug fixes; they often contain crucial security patches that close attack vectors. Ignoring them leaves systems exposed to known exploits, a gift for opportunistic attackers. Defenders must prioritize these updates, understanding that every unpatched system is a potential entry point.

Attackers are constantly scanning for unpatched systems. Their calculus is simple: why burn zero-days when old vulnerabilities are still viable? These cumulative updates from Microsoft directly counter that strategy by patching a broad range of issues. Implement them promptly to reduce your attack surface.
