Cybersecurity news outlet 'חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה' is highlighting a concerning trend: a significant rise in the use of infostealers by various attack groups. This surge is directly contributing to widespread attacks targeting companies, organizations, and individual users, often leading to the exfiltration of sensitive data.

In response to this escalating threat, Hudson Rock, a company specializing in monitoring and alerting on infostealer activity, has launched a free service. According to 'חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה', this tool allows users to monitor up to three domains, three email addresses, and three usernames at no cost. The service provides real-time alerts if any of the monitored credentials are leaked via an infostealer, offering a proactive defense against potential account compromise.

'חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה' emphasizes the importance of closing off initial attack vectors before threat actors can exploit them. This free monitoring capability from Hudson Rock presents a valuable opportunity for individuals and organizations to bolster their security posture by staying informed about potential data exposure.
