Public transit in Los Angeles is reeling after a significant cyberattack crippled several systems at LA Metro. According to 'חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה', a newly emerged Iranian threat group is behind the incident, specifically targeting Rockwell industrial control systems. This attack highlights a growing trend of nation-state actors focusing on critical infrastructure and industrial environments.

The implications are serious. Disrupting public transportation can have cascading effects on a city's economy and daily life. The involvement of Rockwell controllers suggests a sophisticated approach, aiming to exploit vulnerabilities in operational technology (OT) that are often less secured than traditional IT networks. 'חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה' notes that CISA issued a warning about such threats just two days prior, underscoring the immediate and evolving nature of this risk.
