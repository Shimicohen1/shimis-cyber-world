A recent analysis of 216 million security findings across 250 organizations over a 90-day period reveals a stark acceleration in critical risk. According to The Hacker News, while the raw volume of security alerts increased by 52% year-over-year, the more concerning metric — *prioritized critical risk* — skyrocketed by nearly 400%.

This alarming trend, as reported by The Hacker News, is attributed to a growing "velocity gap." The surge in AI-assisted development is enabling faster code generation, but it's also inadvertently creating a higher density of critical vulnerabilities. Essentially, the speed of development is outpacing the ability to secure it, leading to a significant increase in high-impact flaws making their way into production.
