As geopolitical tensions escalate, the cyber domain is increasingly becoming a battleground, and industrial control systems (ICS) are squarely in the crosshairs. Cyber Threat Intelligence reports that these critical systems, vital for manufacturing, energy, and infrastructure, continue to present significant vulnerabilities. This ongoing exposure means that disruptions or compromises could have far-reaching real-world consequences, impacting essential services and supply chains.

The convergence of physical and digital conflict highlights a persistent challenge for security professionals. Exploitable weaknesses in ICS environments can be leveraged by state-sponsored actors or sophisticated criminal groups for disruption, espionage, or even sabotage. The reliance on legacy systems, often difficult to patch or update, coupled with the inherent complexity of operational technology (OT) environments, creates a fertile ground for attackers seeking to cause maximum impact.
