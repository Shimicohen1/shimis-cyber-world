Hold onto your hats, folks. Cyber Threat Intelligence has dropped a bombshell, reporting that Anthropic's AI model, Claude, codenamed 'Mythos', has sniffed out a staggering number of zero-day vulnerabilities. We're talking thousands, impacting major systems across the board. This isn't just some theoretical exercise; these are real, exploitable flaws that attackers would kill for. The sheer scale of the discovery is frankly eye-watering and points to a massive blind spot in our current security postures, particularly within cloud environments.

This AI-driven discovery highlights a critical shift in the threat landscape. While human researchers have always been the frontline defense, AI is clearly emerging as a powerful, albeit potentially double-edged, sword. Cyber Threat Intelligence's findings suggest that these AI models can process vast datasets and identify complex patterns that might elude human analysts for much longer. The implication? Attackers could also be leveraging similar AI capabilities to find these zero-days, making proactive defense more crucial than ever.
