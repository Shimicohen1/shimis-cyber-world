Cyber Threat Intelligence reports that Adobe has issued an emergency patch for a critical zero-day vulnerability affecting Acrobat and Reader. Tracked as CVE-2026-34621, the flaw has been actively exploited in the wild since at least December. The vulnerability allows threat actors to bypass sandbox restrictions, enabling the execution of privileged JavaScript APIs. This, in turn, can lead to arbitrary code execution and the theft of sensitive local files, requiring only the opening of a malicious PDF.

The exploit leverages specific APIs like `util.readFileIntoStream()` for unauthorized file access and `RSS.addFeed()` for data exfiltration and downloading additional malicious payloads. Security researcher Haifei Li discovered the issue after analyzing a sample dubbed "yummy_adobe_exploit_uwu.pdf." The sample, submitted to VirusTotal in late March, initially evaded detection by most security vendors, highlighting the stealthy nature of the exploit.

Further analysis revealed that attacks in the wild, observed by researcher Gi7w0rm, utilized Russian-language documents with lures targeting the oil and gas industry. Adobe's rapid response, following Li's report, underscores the severity and active exploitation of this vulnerability.
