Digital attacks are increasingly fueling a surge in cargo theft, with losses in North America projected to hit a staggering $6.6 billion by 2025, according to fleet management company Geotab. The Record by Recorded Future recently highlighted how cybercriminals are employing sophisticated remote access campaigns to orchestrate these thieving operations, moving far beyond traditional smash-and-grab tactics.

This shift underscores a worrying trend: the convergence of physical logistics and cyber exploitation. It's no longer just about brute force; these threat actors are leveraging digital vulnerabilities to gain control, disrupt supply chains, and ultimately make off with valuable cargo. The sophistication involved suggests well-resourced groups are behind these campaigns, indicating a lucrative, low-risk avenue for illicit gains.
