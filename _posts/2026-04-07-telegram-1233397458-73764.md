The FBI's latest Internet Crime Report paints a grim picture, revealing a staggering $20 billion lost to cybercrime in the past year. Pentesting News highlights that a significant driver behind this surge is the escalating sophistication and prevalence of AI-powered and investment-related scams. These aren't your grandpa's phishing emails; attackers are leveraging cutting-edge tech to craft more convincing lures and exploit financial aspirations with alarming efficiency.

The report, as detailed by Pentesting News, points to a clear trend: cybercriminals are adapting rapidly, integrating AI into their operations for everything from generating hyper-personalized social engineering attacks to automating fraudulent investment schemes. This convergence of AI and financial fraud represents a new frontier in cyber threats, making it harder for individuals and organizations alike to distinguish legitimate opportunities from malicious traps. The sheer scale of financial losses underscores the urgent need for enhanced defenses and heightened user awareness.
