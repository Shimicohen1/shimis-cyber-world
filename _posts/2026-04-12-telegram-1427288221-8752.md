A recent court case in Texas highlighted a critical privacy caveat for Signal users: the FBI successfully recovered deleted Signal messages by leveraging iPhone notification history. According to חדשות סייבר - ארז דסה, an FBI representative explained that even after the defendant deleted the Signal application, the operating system retained previews of incoming messages within the notification history. This allowed investigators to reconstruct conversations that were thought to be permanently erased.

The FBI's access to this data was reportedly facilitated by Israeli forensics firm Cellebrite's technology, a known player in mobile device extraction. This revelation underscores that while Signal provides robust end-to-end encryption for message transmission, the device's operating system can still create persistent local records. These records, often overlooked, can become a significant point of exposure if a device is compromised or legally seized.
