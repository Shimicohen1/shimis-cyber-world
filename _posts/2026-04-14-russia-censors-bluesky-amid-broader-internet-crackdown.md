Russia's digital landscape continues to shrink, with the social media platform Bluesky now reportedly added to the country's registry of banned websites. This move, reported by The Record by Recorded Future, comes as Russia's communications watchdog, Roskomnadzor, intensifies its internet restrictions.

The addition of Bluesky to the banned list signifies a continued effort by Russian authorities to control information flow and limit access to platforms not operating under their direct oversight. For users within Russia, this means yet another avenue for open communication and information exchange has been effectively shut down, pushing them towards state-sanctioned alternatives or requiring the use of VPNs to circumvent blocks.

This isn't just about one social media platform; it’s part of a larger, more aggressive trend. The Record by Recorded Future’s sources indicate this is a calculated escalation in Russia's ongoing campaign to tighten its grip on internet freedom, creating a more isolated digital environment for its citizens. It’s a stark reminder of how quickly digital rights can erode under authoritarian regimes.
