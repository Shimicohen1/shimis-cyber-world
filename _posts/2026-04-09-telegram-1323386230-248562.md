Cyber Threat Intelligence is flagging a significant shift in how the ClickFix malware operates on macOS. Traditionally, such threats rely on subtlety to infiltrate systems. However, this evolving attack vector has recently demonstrated a more brazen approach, making a notable change to its distribution strategy that immediately caught the attention of researchers.

According to Cyber Threat Intelligence, the malware is no longer attempting to blend in. Instead, it's adopting a more aggressive tactic designed to spread its malicious payload. This departure from typical stealth techniques is a clear indicator that attackers are adapting their methods, potentially in response to evolving defenses or to exploit new vulnerabilities. The specific nature of this 'major change' suggests a calculated risk to achieve wider infection rates, even if it means being more detectable.
