The cybersecurity landscape is shifting at an unprecedented pace, largely driven by the rapid integration of Artificial Intelligence. What used to be a steady, incremental evolution of threats and defenses is now a whirlwind, forcing security professionals to adapt or get left behind. This isn't just theoretical; it's a fundamental change in the game.

Tools and techniques that were once the exclusive domain of nation-state actors or highly sophisticated cybercriminal rings are becoming democratized. AI-powered capabilities can drastically reduce the time and expertise needed to discover vulnerabilities, craft sophisticated phishing campaigns, or even develop novel attack vectors. This means the window of opportunity for defenders to patch, detect, and respond is shrinking dramatically.
