The UK's media regulator, Ofcom, has launched an investigation into Telegram. This probe stems from information provided by the Canadian Centre for Child Protection, which reportedly indicated the presence and distribution of Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM) on the platform. This action signals a growing global focus on how large communication platforms handle illegal content.

While the specific details of the information shared by the Canadian Centre remain undisclosed, the investigation by Ofcom is a significant step. It places Telegram under scrutiny for its content moderation policies and the platform's role in facilitating the spread of harmful material. Defenders should be aware of increased regulatory pressure on communication tools that lack robust content filtering.
