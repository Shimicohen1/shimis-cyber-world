According to a recent report by Cyber Threat Intelligence, Americans are losing billions of dollars annually to a surge in AI and cryptocurrency-related scams. This isn't just about small-time phishing; we're talking sophisticated social engineering campaigns leveraging the hype around new tech to fleece unsuspecting victims.

The FBI's latest figures underscore a worrying trend: threat actors are increasingly weaponizing AI's perceived legitimacy and the allure of quick crypto gains. These scams often start with convincing deepfake audio or video, or investment schemes promising unrealistic returns, drawing victims into what appears to be a legitimate opportunity. Once trust is established, the financial drain begins, often leaving victims with significant losses and little recourse. It's a stark reminder that even with advanced tech, the oldest tricks in the book—greed and manipulation—are still the most effective.
